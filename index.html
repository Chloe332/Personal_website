About me

I’m Chloe Hallaert, an Honors Software Engineering student at the University of Waterloo from the United States with a passion for harnessing AI and software engineering to solve real-world problems. My experience spans diverse AI-driven projects, including building abstract retrieval APIs, deploying Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipelines with vector databases, and developing object detection systems for autonomous navigation.

Beyond software, I have led hands-on engineering projects, including leading a team of peers for the NASA Human Exploration Rover Challenge. We designed, built, and presented our rover and design report to NASA engineers, an experience that honed both my technical and leadership skills.

My technical expertise spans programming languages like Python, C, C++, and HTML, as well as tools and frameworks like Docker, AWS, TensorFlow, ROS2, YOLOv5, and Pandas.

In my free time, I enjoy teaching, such as being a math and English tutor for kids. I enjoy to dance, drawing, learning new languages, and writing books. In fact, I'm actually a published author of two educational children's books!
